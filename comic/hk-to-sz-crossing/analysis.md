# Content Analysis — HK to Shenzhen Border Crossing

## Source
`content/guides/hong-kong-to-shenzhen.md`

## Core Message
Step-by-step practical guide for Western tourists crossing the Hong Kong–Shenzhen border for the first time. Reduces anxiety by making the unfamiliar process feel clear and manageable.

## Target Audience
Western tourists (US, UK, Europe, Australia) aged 20–50, visiting Shenzhen from Hong Kong. English-speaking. First-timers at a Chinese land border crossing. Slightly nervous but curious and adventurous.

## Narrative Arc
Nervous stranger → informed traveler → confident arrival. The emotional journey is: anxiety about the unknown → step-by-step clarity → relief and excitement upon arrival.

## Key Moments to Illustrate
1. **Cover**: Traveler about to cross — the moment of anticipation
2. **Step 1**: HK Immigration — showing passport, exit stamp
3. **Step 2**: Walking the crossing corridor — seeing Shenzhen ahead
4. **Step 3**: China Immigration + digital arrival card on phone
5. **Step 4**: Biometric scan — fingerprints, calm and routine
6. **Step 5**: Arrival in Shenzhen metro — relief, excitement, freedom

## Style Signals
- Educational + narrative (ligne-claire optimal)
- Warm and reassuring, not intimidating (warm tone)
- Must be internationally readable — no text in panels (images only)
- Consistent character across all 6 images

## Art + Tone Decision
- **Art**: ligne-claire (clean lines, flat colors, Tintin-style — universally legible, editorial feel matches site)
- **Tone**: warm (golden light, amber accents, cream backgrounds — matches site's Ink & Amber design system)
- **Layout**: standard (individual panels, 4:3 landscape, embed directly in article)
- **Aspect**: 4:3 (landscape, better for article inline display)
